Most program managers still prefer to use Microsoft Excel to analyze data and make decisions. In protection programming, much of incident-based data is highly confidential and cannot be shared without informed consent and a data protection policy detailing the scope. The Somalia protection monitoring system however is not an incident-based system but a community recall of occurrence of specific protection concerns. It aggregates these responses into a % and shows trends. There are different sections:

  • Trends show how a community recalls the occurrence of protection concerns over time
  • Prevalence indicates how often such violations of these protection concerns happens
  • Most affected groups shows the most affected persons in the community
  • Connectors and dividers shows people who assist communities while dividers show spoilers

A plethora of data were acquired and analyzed to create the Intelligentsia model. Among those data sets are:

  • Retail environment data: Yelp ratings
  • Transportation data: NYC TLC taxicab trips, NYC Subway network and stations, residents’ travel time to work
  • Quality of life: Number and location of trees (NYC Open Data), Crime complaints (NYPD)
  • Online data: Edits of Wikipedia neighborhood articles, Google Search Trends
  • Housing: Median home value, gross rent, year structure built (Zillow, ACS/Census)
  • Demographics: Median income, age, education, poverty, employment (ACS/Census)
